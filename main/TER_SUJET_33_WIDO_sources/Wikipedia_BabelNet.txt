BabelNet

 is a 

multilingual

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multilinguality

 lexical-semantic 

knowledge graph

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_graph

, 

ontology

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ontology_(information_science)

 and encyclopedic 

dictionary

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dictionary

 developed at the 

NLP

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_Language_Processing

 group of the 

Sapienza University of Rome

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sapienza_University_of_Rome

 under the supervision of 

Roberto Navigli

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roberto_Navigli

.

[ 1 ]

[ 2 ]

 BabelNet was automatically created by linking 

Wikipedia

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia

 to the most popular computational 

lexicon

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dictionary

 of the 

English language

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_language

, 

WordNet

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WordNet

. The integration is done using an automatic mapping and by filling in lexical gaps in resource-poor 

languages

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language

 by using 

statistical machine translation

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_machine_translation

. The result is an 

encyclopedic dictionary

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encyclopedic_dictionary

 that provides 

concepts

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concept

 and 

named entities

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Named_entity

lexicalized

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Named_entity

 in many languages and connected with large amounts of 

semantic relations

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantic_relation#Relationships

. Additional lexicalizations and definitions are added by linking to free-license wordnets, OmegaWiki, the English 

Wiktionary

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wiktionary

, 

Wikidata

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikidata

, 

FrameNet

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FrameNet

, 

VerbNet

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VerbNet

 and others. Similarly to WordNet, BabelNet groups 

words

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word

 in different languages into sets of 

synonyms

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synonyms

, called 

Babel 

synsets

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synsets

. For each Babel synset, BabelNet provides short definitions (called 

glosses

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Definition

) in many languages harvested from both WordNet and Wikipedia. 

BabelNet is a multilingual semantic network obtained as an integration of WordNet and Wikipedia.  

Statistics of BabelNet

[

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=BabelNet&action=edit&section=1

]  

As of December 2023

[update]

, BabelNet (version 5.3) covers 600 

languages

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language

. It contains almost 23 million synsets and around 1.7 billion 

word senses

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word_sense

 (regardless of their language). Each Babel synset contains 2 synonyms per language, i.e., word senses, on average. The semantic network includes all the lexico-semantic relations from WordNet (

hypernymy and hyponymy

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyponymy

, 

meronymy

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meronymy

 and 

holonymy

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holonymy

, 

antonymy

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antonymy

 and 

synonymy

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synonymy

, etc., totaling around 364,000 relation edges) as well as an underspecified relatedness relation from Wikipedia (totaling around 1.9 billion edges).

[ 1 ]

 Version 5.3 also associates around 61 million images with Babel synsets and provides a Lemon 

RDF

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resource_Description_Framework

 encoding of the resource,

[ 3 ]

 available via a 

SPARQL endpoint

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SPARQL_endpoint

. 2.67 million synsets are assigned domain labels. 

Applications

[

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=BabelNet&action=edit&section=2

]  

BabelNet has been shown to enable multilingual 

natural language processing

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_language_processing

 applications. The lexicalized 

knowledge

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge

 available in BabelNet has been shown to obtain state-of-the-art results in: 

Semantic relatedness

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge

,

[ 4 ]

[ 5 ]

Multilingual 

word-sense disambiguation

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word-sense_disambiguation

[ 6 ]

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Word-sense_disambiguation

 and 

entity linking

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entity_linking

, with the 

Babelfy

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babelfy

 system,

[ 7 ]

Video games with a purpose

.

Prizes and acknowledgments

[

edit

https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=BabelNet&action=edit&section=3

]  

BabelNet received the META prize 2015 for "groundbreaking work in overcoming language barriers through a multilingual lexicalised semantic network and ontology making use of heterogeneous data sources". 

The Artificial Intelligence Journal paper that describes BabelNet

[ 1 ]

 won the Prominent Paper Award in 2017.

[ 9 ]

BabelNet featured prominently in a 

Time

 magazine article

[ 10 ]

 about the new age of innovative and up-to-date lexical knowledge resources available on the Web.
